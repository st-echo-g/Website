Welcome to Echo's Site

I'm a Boston resident and PhD candidate in the Mechanical Engineering program at Northeastern University, where I study/research carbon-free combustion through iron powder, a project that spans multiple labs, universities, and continents. I focus on the variables that change the ignition temperature of iron powder, including how that temperature shifts with powder morphology, and I examine combustion byproducts for future reuse/reduction through the iron fuel cycle, along with the potentially useful properties of the nanoparticles that form around the micrometric particles during combustion.

Outside the lab, I support other graduate students in sharing their research as a Communication Lab Fellow, present to high school and undergraduate students through STEM outreach, and volunteer with a few local groups. I like working alongside people who care about expanding educational opportunities and/or climate advocacy that includes social justice. If any of this sounds interesting, reach out at . I'd love to talk about some of the groups doing this work in Boston and how I might be able to help.

Beyond all of that, I paint/garden/take photos/boardgames/videogames. More than one mentee has called me "artsy," which probably shows in this website: part professional portfolio, part space for me to organize and catalog my own projects/thoughts.

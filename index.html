Hi, welcome to my website. I'm Echo St. Germain, a PhD candidate at Northeastern University studying iron particle ignition and combustion, exploring iron as a zero-carbon, recyclable fuel. My research looks at how oxide layers form on iron particles during storage, how furnace heating rates affect ignitability, and what conditions lead to complete combustion. Outside the lab I work as a Communication Lab Fellow at Northeastern where I support graduate students with research posters and figures. I also take part in STEM outreach across the Boston area and volunteer with local organizations where my skills can be used.
